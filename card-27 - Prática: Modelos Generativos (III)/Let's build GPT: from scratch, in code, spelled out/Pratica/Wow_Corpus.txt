INTRODUCTION
Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Games (MMORPGs) have developed into complex digital
ecosystems where narrative depth and interactive engagement shape player experience. In these large virtual
worlds, storytelling not only builds the world but also improves immersion, emotional involvement, and a sense
of agency. Despite their potential, most MMORPGs stick to pre-written quest lines, fixed dialogue options, and
scripted events that do not respond to player actions or the changing state of the game world. This static way of
delivering narratives limits responsiveness and restricts player-driven storytelling. It often results in repetitive or
uninteresting content in large environments. These issues highlight the need for more flexible and aware narrative
systems that can showcase the richness of player interaction in real time.
Recent advancements in large language models (LLMs) have opened new possibilities for natural language
generation in interactive systems, particularly those requiring context sensitivity and narrative continuity. While
LLMs such as GPT-based architectures have demonstrated impressive capabilities in dialogue generation, story
synthesis, and text-based role-playing, their application within dynamic, multiplayer game environments remains
underexplored. Existing implementations of narrative generation often focus on single-player or controlled
sandbox settings, lacking the scalability and adaptability required for real-time interaction with multiple players
in evolving game states. This study aims to investigate the integration of LLMs into an adaptive storytelling
framework specifically designed for MMORPGs, addressing how such models can generate thematically
coherent, lore-consistent, and contextually appropriate narratives in response to diverse player interactions.
This study proposes a structured approach to integrating large language models into adaptive narrative systems
within MMORPG environments. It focuses on the design and implementation of a storytelling framework based
on a fine-tuned LLM trained on a comprehensive World of Warcraft (WoW) corpus, including thousands of quest
narratives, dialogues, item descriptions, and lore texts. Unlike traditional rule-based or template-driven methods,
our approach enables the generation of dynamic, context-aware narratives that respond to diverse player
interactions. By simulating varied gameplay scenarios and narrative themes, the study explores how LLMs can
support flexible story progression while maintaining coherence with game lore and thematic intent. In doing so,
this work contributes a scalable methodology for deploying AI-driven storytelling systems in large-scale
multiplayer contexts, bridging a gap between static content delivery and emergent narrative generation.
The remainder of this paper is structured as follows. The next section presents the theoretical framework,
reviewing key literature on narrative generation, adaptive storytelling, and the use of large language models in
interactive systems. This is followed by the Method section, which details the data collection process,
preprocessing steps, fine-tuning configuration, and evaluation design. The Results section reports the performance
metrics of the model across multiple narrative dimensions and highlights key findings from expert assessments
and user studies. In the final sections, the Conclusion summarizes the main contributions of the study, while the
Discussion and Recommendations section offers a critical reflection on the limitations of the current approach
and outlines directions for future research in AI-assisted storytelling within complex, multiplayer environments.
1. RELATED WORKS
Narrative systems in digital games have undergone significant evolution, from linear storytelling formats to more
complex branching structures designed to simulate interactivity. Early implementations of game narratives
typically followed fixed, pre-authored scripts that offered little variation or responsiveness to player actions.
While branching dialogue trees and modular quest architectures introduced a degree of customization, they
remained inherently limited in scalability and adaptability, especially in open-world or multiplayer contexts
(Mateas & Stern, 2005; Aarseth, 2012). In many commercial game engines, narrative logic is still hardcoded into
design frameworks, requiring extensive manual authoring and predefined decision paths. As a result, narrative
design often becomes a bottleneck in delivering truly dynamic and player-driven story experiences, particularly
in genres like MMORPGs where vast content and sustained engagement are required.

Adaptive storytelling has evolved substantially with the integration of artificial intelligence, moving beyond
traditional rule-based frameworks. Early systems
such as drama managers and finite-state narrative engines
provided limited interactivity and rigid dialogue capabilities (Riedl & Bulitko, 2013; Fairclough, 2004). More
recent advances leverage generative AI to enhance real-time flexibility and personalization in game narratives.
Ratican and Hutson (2024) introduced Adaptive Worlds, demonstrating how generative AI can dynamically tailor
content to gameplay stimuli. Jeong and Lee (2025) proposed the LIGS framework, embedding GPT-style large
language models into game systems to support emergent and context-sensitive narrative structures. Summers-
Stay and Voss (2024) developed methods for branching and converging storylines in multiplayer environments,
tackling a key challenge in collaborative narrative coherence. While these contributions signal a paradigm shift
toward intelligent narrative systems, their applicability to large-scale MMORPG settings
with demands for
thematic consistency, lore fidelity, and multi-player responsiveness
remains insufficiently explored.
Large language models (LLMs) have become prominent tools in natural language generation tasks, including
dialogue systems, story continuation, and character simulation. Models such as GPT-3 and its successors have
demonstrated remarkable capabilities in generating coherent and contextually relevant text across a variety of
domains (Brown et al., 2020; Radford et al., 2020). In the domain of interactive storytelling, Ammanabrolu et al.
(2021) introduced techniques for fine-tuning LLMs on game-like text corpora, enabling dynamic narrative
responses in fantasy role-playing settings. Similarly, Alabdulkarim et al. (2021) leveraged reinforcement learning
to align model outputs with desired story structures, enhancing consistency and player relevance. Although LLMs
have been tested in single-player and turn-based scenarios, their deployment in real-time, multiplayer contexts
where interactions are asynchronous, overlapping, and semantically complex
poses unique challenges. Issues
such as character voice retention, lore fidelity, and thematic alignment become more pronounced when multiple
users interact with a shared narrative space, an area that remains underrepresented in existing research (Urbanek
et al., 2019).
Despite the rapid progress in LLM-driven storytelling for single-player and turn-based games, significantly less
attention has been devoted to large-scale, real-time multiplayer environments like MMORPGs. Systems such as
NarrativeGenie demonstrate the potential for dynamic narrative sequencing in game-like contexts (Kumuran et
al., 2024), while qualitative analyses point out the need for context-aware NPC dialogue and background lore
integration (van der Torre, 2025). Still, these implementations predominantly target structured or text-based
scenarios rather than persistent worlds shared by hundreds or thousands of players. Fundamental challenges
such as maintaining character voice across simultaneous player interactions, ensuring lore fidelity under evolving
game conditions, and achieving low-latency narrative responses
remain largely unaddressed. This study situates
itself within this understudied niche, aiming to investigate how fine-tuned LLMs can generate thematically
consistent and responsive narratives in an active MMORPG setting based on a comprehensive World of Warcraft
corpus.

2. METHODOLOGY
This study adopts a case study methodology to investigate the application of large language models (LLMs) in
adaptive narrative generation within the context of a massively multiplayer online role-playing game
(MMORPG). The experimental setting was designed around World of Warcraft (WoW), a well-established game
universe with a rich narrative infrastructure and extensive player interaction dynamics. The objective was to
evaluate how a fine-tuned LLM can generate thematically consistent and context-sensitive narratives in response
to varied player inputs. To this end, a state-of-the-art open-source model, LLaMA 2 13B developed by Meta AI,
was selected as the base architecture due to its balance between performance, adaptability, and computational
efficiency for custom fine-tuning.
The fine-tuning corpus was compiled from publicly available, officially licensed textual materials from the World
of Warcraft (WoW) universe. The dataset consisted of approximately 1.2 million tokens in total, drawn from a
wide range of narrative assets including 15,000 quest texts, 25,000 character dialogues, 3,500 item descriptions,


and 800 lore documents. These materials were selected to capture the linguistic style, thematic structures, and
world-specific terminology characteristic of the WoW narrative domain. Prior to training, the data underwent a
multi-stage preprocessing pipeline, including the removal of duplicate entries, normalization of punctuation and
formatting, and the contextual segmentation of long documents into coherent, semantically unified passages
suitable for sequence-based fine-tuning. This process ensured both thematic integrity and syntactic consistency
in the resulting training dataset.
The selected model, LLaMA 2 13B by Meta AI, was fine-tuned using the preprocessed World of Warcraft corpus
to align its generative capabilities with the stylistic and semantic expectations of the game world. The fine-tuning
process was conducted using a learning rate of 2e-5 and a batch size of 16, over the course of 1,000 training
epochs. Training was performed on high-performance GPU infrastructure to support large-batch gradient
computation and to maintain stable convergence across extended training cycles. The choice of a relatively low
learning rate and prolonged training duration was intended to allow the model to internalize subtle narrative
conventions without overfitting to repetitive patterns. Throughout the training process, checkpoints were
evaluated using held-out validation subsets to monitor coherence and overfitting behavior. The final model
checkpoint was selected based on a balance of narrative fluency, consistency of style, and lore alignment in
preliminary outputs.
To assess the performance of the fine-tuned model in a simulated MMORPG environment, a set of 50 player
interaction scenarios was constructed. These scenarios were designed to span 10 distinct narrative themes
commonly encountered in role-playing game contexts, including combat, diplomacy, exploration, quest
progression, moral choice, alliance formation, betrayal, world-building, conflict resolution, and character
development. Each scenario prompted the model to generate narrative responses based on player input and
situational cues. Evaluation was conducted using a mixed-methods approach combining expert annotation and
user-based scoring. Thematic consistency and lore adherence were assessed by two domain experts with narrative
design experience, while narrative coherence was rated on a 5-point Likert scale by 30 independent participants
with familiarity in fantasy storytelling. Additionally, latency metrics were recorded for each response to evaluate
real-time feasibility. This evaluation protocol was designed to reflect both subjective narrative quality and system-
level performance constraints.

4. CONCLUSION
This study explored the integration of large language models (LLMs), specifically LLaMA 2 13B, into the
narrative generation pipeline of a massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) environment. By
fine-tuning on a domain-specific corpus comprising over 1.2 million tokens from World of Warcraft, the model
demonstrated strong performance in multiple narrative quality metrics. Results indicate high thematic consistency
across diverse narrative themes (87.3%), substantial narrative coherence as rated by participants (4.5/5), and
strong adherence to in-universe lore (92.1%). Additionally, the average response generation latency of 480 ms
suggests the feasibility of deploying such models in real-time multiplayer settings. These findings collectively
ility to generate contextually relevant and immersive narratives while meeting the
responsiveness demands of live game environments.
Despite these promising results, several limitations remain. While the model maintained a high degree of lore
fidelity, error analysis revealed challenges in preserving character-specific voice (14%) and factual consistency
(8%). Furthermore, although the latency figures were within acceptable real-time thresholds, they may fluctuate
under higher player loads or more complex interaction chains. Another constraint is the static nature of the fine-
tuning corpus, which may limit adaptability to evolving narratives or user-generated content over time. These
limitations underscore the need for ongoing model updates, hybrid retrieval-generation architectures, and context
caching mechanisms to ensure sustained performance and narrative alignment in dynamic multiplayer
environments.
Future research should explore adaptive memory mechanisms and real-time context retrieval to further enhance
narrative coherence and responsiveness in multiplayer settings. Integrating retrieval-augmented generation
(RAG) pipelines with dynamically indexed lore databases could help sustain consistency as in-game events
evolve. Additionally, incorporating reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) tailored to thematic
alignment and player satisfaction may further improve narrative quality. Expanding evaluation frameworks to
include player sentiment analysis and longitudinal engagement metrics would also provide a deeper understanding
of narrative impact. Overall, this study serves as a foundation for scalable, AI-driven storytelling systems in
persistent online worlds.
